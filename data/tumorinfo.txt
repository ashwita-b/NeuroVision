What is the brain?
The brain is the central organ of the nervous system responsible for cognition, movement, sensation, memory, emotions, and vital life functions such as breathing and heart rate.

What are the main parts of the brain?
The main parts of the brain are the cerebrum, cerebellum, and brainstem.

What is a brain tumor?
A brain tumor is an abnormal growth of cells in or around the brain that disrupts normal brain function.

What is glioma tumor?
A glioma is a type of brain tumor that originates from glial cells, which support and protect neurons in the brain.

Are all brain tumors cancerous?
No, brain tumors can be benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous).

What causes brain tumors?
The exact cause is often unknown, but factors may include genetic mutations, radiation exposure, and metastasis from other cancers.

What is a meningioma tumor?
A meningioma is a tumor arising from the meninges, the protective layers surrounding the brain and spinal cord, and is usually slow-growing and benign.

Where do meningiomas commonly occur?
Meningiomas commonly occur on the surface of the brain near the skull.

What symptoms are associated with meningioma?
Symptoms may include headaches, seizures, vision problems, and weakness depending on the tumor’s location.

What is a pituitary tumor?
A pituitary tumor is an abnormal growth in the pituitary gland that affects hormone production and regulation in the body.

Are pituitary tumors dangerous?
Most pituitary tumors are benign, but they can cause serious hormonal imbalances if untreated.

What symptoms are caused by pituitary tumors?
Symptoms include headaches, vision problems, fatigue, hormonal imbalance, weight changes, and growth disorders.

Are gliomas aggressive?
Many gliomas are malignant and aggressive, especially high-grade gliomas such as glioblastoma.

What are common symptoms of glioma?
Symptoms include persistent headaches, seizures, difficulty speaking, memory loss, and motor weakness.

What does no tumor mean?
No tumor means that imaging scans show no abnormal mass or tumor in the brain.

Can a person have symptoms without a brain tumor?
Yes, symptoms like headaches or dizziness can occur due to stress, infections, migraines, or other non-tumor conditions.

Why is early brain tumor detection important?
Early detection improves treatment options, reduces complications, and increases survival rates.

How are brain tumors detected?
Brain tumors are commonly detected using MRI or CT scans and confirmed through medical evaluation.

What is BERT?
BERT is a transformer-based NLP model that learns contextual meaning of words using both left and right context.

Why is BERT called bidirectional?
It reads text in both directions, allowing better understanding of word meaning in context.

What problem did BERT solve in NLP?
It improved contextual understanding compared to unidirectional language models.

Why is standard BERT inefficient for similarity search?
It requires pairwise comparisons, which is slow for large document sets.

What is SBERT?
SBERT is a modified BERT model designed to generate fixed-length sentence embeddings efficiently.

How does SBERT differ from BERT?
SBERT encodes sentences independently instead of comparing them pairwise.

Why is SBERT better for chatbots?
It enables fast semantic similarity computation for real-time responses.

What is an embedding?
An embedding is a numerical vector representation of text that captures semantic meaning.

What is the standard treatment for glioma?
Treatment usually involves surgical removal of the tumor when possible, followed by radiation therapy and chemotherapy to reduce recurrence and manage tumor growth.

Can gliomas be completely cured?
Complete cure is challenging for high-grade gliomas due to aggressive growth, but low-grade gliomas may be managed effectively with surgery and therapy.

Are there new therapies for glioma?
Targeted therapies, immunotherapy, and clinical trials using novel drugs are being explored to improve outcomes for glioma patients.

How are meningiomas treated?
Small or asymptomatic meningiomas may be monitored with regular imaging, while symptomatic or large tumors are usually treated with surgical removal and, in some cases, radiation therapy.

Can meningiomas come back after surgery?
Yes, recurrence is possible, especially if the tumor was not completely removed. Regular follow-up scans are recommended.

Is radiation therapy effective for meningiomas?
Yes, radiation therapy can help control growth for tumors that cannot be fully removed surgically or for recurrent meningiomas.

How are pituitary tumors treated?
Pituitary tumors are treated with surgery, medication to control hormone levels, and sometimes radiation therapy depending on the tumor type and size.

Can pituitary tumors affect hormones?
Yes, pituitary tumors can overproduce or reduce hormone levels, leading to various symptoms such as growth problems, vision changes, or metabolic issues.

Are pituitary tumors always cancerous?
Most pituitary tumors are benign, but they can still cause significant symptoms due to pressure on surrounding brain structures or hormone imbalance.

What are the main treatment options for brain tumors?
Brain tumor treatment may include surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, targeted therapy, and supportive care to manage symptoms.

How treatment plan decided for brain tumor?
Treatment depends on the tumor type, size, location, grade, patient age, and overall health, usually determined by a multidisciplinary medical team.

Can lifestyle changes help brain tumor treatment?
While lifestyle changes cannot cure brain tumors, healthy nutrition, exercise as tolerated, and stress management can support overall well-being during treatment.

Why is a retrieval-based chatbot used?
Retrieval-based chatbots provide precise, pre-verified answers from a trusted database, reducing the risk of incorrect or unsafe information in sensitive fields like medicine.

Why not LLM like ChatGPT ?

Are retrieval-based chatbots faster than LLMs?
Yes, they often provide answers faster because they fetch pre-written responses instead of generating text dynamically, reducing processing time.

Can retrieval-based handle medical queries?
